# Press inquiry response &mdash; template

This template covers the first response to a journalist's inquiry. Reply within **4 working hours** during EEST business hours, **within 12 hours otherwise**. Always reply in the language of the inquiry; if unclear, default to English. For Romanian-language outlets, switch to Romanian. For Russian-language outlets (RU/MD), switch to Russian.

The template is intentionally short. The goal of the first email is not to give the interview &mdash; it is to lock the interview slot and to send a press kit.

---

## D. Internal triage notes

- **Always log the inquiry** in `crm/press-inquiries.csv` (or Cronberry tag `press-inquiry-md-chat-2026`) with: date, outlet, journalist name, language, angle, deadline, slot picked, attachments sent.
- **Tier-1 outlets** (Politico EU, The Register, Heise, FT, Bloomberg, Le Monde, Reuters, Wired): reply within 2 hours, escalate to Oleg directly.
- **Tier-2 outlets** (EURACTIV, EUobserver, ZDNet, TechCrunch EU, Sifted, ipn.md, NewsMaker, Ziarul de Gardă): reply within 4 hours.
- **Tier-3 outlets** (everything else, including blogs and tech newsletters): reply within 24 hours, send press kit only.
- **Hostile or sceptical framings**: respond with the same template &mdash; never lower the temperature by skipping facts. The press kit is the same regardless of the angle.
- **Off-record requests**: politely decline. We do not do off-record at launch. Everything is on-record or no comment.
- **„Why Moldova" question**: prepared one-liner &mdash; "Because we are an EU candidate without a sovereign messenger, because IT Park gives us a clean legal and tax footprint, and because waiting for someone else to build it is no longer a responsible position in 2026."
- **„Will you sell to the Moldovan state" question**: prepared one-liner &mdash; "We are an SRL, not a state vendor. If the state wants to license the software, the code is AGPLv3 and they are welcome. We do not pursue government customers as a primary go-to-market."
- **„Backdoor for Moldovan or EU authorities" question**: prepared one-liner &mdash; "Technically impossible. We do not hold message keys. Lawful-access cooperation is limited to metadata and account information; the code is public and the cryptography is standard and audited."
